★ Key Formulas for Section 6.1
Arc Length
$$s = r\theta \qquad (\theta \text{ must be in radians})$$ To find any one of $s$, $r$, $\theta$: rearrange as $r = s/\theta$ or $\theta = s/r$.
Area of a Sector
$$A = \tfrac{1}{2}r^2\theta \qquad (\theta \text{ in radians})$$ Solve for $r$: $r = \sqrt{2A/\theta}$  ·  Solve for $\theta$: $\theta = 2A/r^2$
Degree → Radian Conversion
$$\theta_{\text{rad}} = \theta_{\deg} \times \frac{\pi}{180}$$ Common: $30° = \pi/6$, $\;60° = \pi/3$, $\;90° = \pi/2$, $\;120° = 2\pi/3$, $\;180° = \pi$

Problem 71

Arc Length
Given
$r = 10$ meters, $\;\theta = \dfrac{1}{2}$ radian.   Find $s$.
Step 1 — Apply the arc-length formula
$$s = r\theta$$
Step 2 — Substitute
$$s = 10 \times \frac{1}{2} = 5 \text{ meters}$$
Final Answer
$$s = 5 \text{ meters}$$

Problem 72

Arc Length
Given
$r = 6$ feet, $\;\theta = 2$ radians.   Find $s$.
Step 1 — Apply $s = r\theta$
$$s = 6 \times 2 = 12 \text{ feet}$$
Final Answer
$$s = 12 \text{ feet}$$

Problem 73

Arc Length
Given
$\theta = \dfrac{1}{4}$ radian, $\;s = 6$ centimeters.   Find $r$.
Step 1 — Rearrange the formula for $r$
$$r = \frac{s}{\theta}$$
Step 2 — Substitute
$$r = \frac{6}{1/4} = 6 \times 4 = 24 \text{ cm}$$
Final Answer
$$r = 24 \text{ centimeters}$$

Problem 74

Arc Length
Given
$\theta = \dfrac{1}{3}$ radian, $\;s = 2$ feet.   Find $r$.
Step 1 — $r = s/\theta$
$$r = \frac{2}{1/3} = 2 \times 3 = 6 \text{ feet}$$
Final Answer
$$r = 6 \text{ feet}$$

Problem 75

Arc Length
Given
$r = 6$ meters, $\;s = 8$ meters.   Find $\theta$.
Step 1 — Rearrange for $\theta$
$$\theta = \frac{s}{r}$$
Step 2 — Substitute
$$\theta = \frac{8}{6} = \frac{4}{3} \approx 1.333 \text{ radians}$$
Final Answer
$$\theta = \frac{4}{3} \approx 1.333 \text{ radians}$$

Problem 76

Arc Length
Given
$r = 5$ miles, $\;s = 3$ miles.   Find $\theta$.
Step 1 — $\theta = s/r$
$$\theta = \frac{3}{5} = 0.6 \text{ radians}$$
Final Answer
$$\theta = \frac{3}{5} = 0.600 \text{ radians}$$

Problem 77

Arc Length — Degree Conversion
Given
$r = 3$ meters, $\;\theta = 120°$.   Find $s$.
Step 1 — Convert degrees to radians
$$\theta = 120° \times \frac{\pi}{180} = \frac{2\pi}{3} \text{ rad}$$
Step 2 — Apply $s = r\theta$
$$s = 3 \times \frac{2\pi}{3} = 2\pi \approx 6.283 \text{ m}$$
Final Answer
$$s = 2\pi \approx 6.283 \text{ meters}$$

Problem 78

Arc Length — Degree Conversion
Given
$r = 2$ inches, $\;\theta = 30°$.   Find $s$.
Step 1 — Convert degrees to radians
$$\theta = 30° \times \frac{\pi}{180} = \frac{\pi}{6} \text{ rad}$$
Step 2 — Apply $s = r\theta$
$$s = 2 \times \frac{\pi}{6} = \frac{\pi}{3} \approx 1.047 \text{ in}$$
Final Answer
$$s = \frac{\pi}{3} \approx 1.047 \text{ inches}$$

Problem 79

Area of Sector
Given
$r = 10$ meters, $\;\theta = \dfrac{1}{2}$ radian.   Find $A$.
Step 1 — Apply the area-of-sector formula
$$A = \frac{1}{2}r^2\theta$$
Step 2 — Substitute
$$A = \frac{1}{2}(10)^2\left(\frac{1}{2}\right) = \frac{1}{2}(100)\left(\frac{1}{2}\right) = 25 \text{ m}^2$$
Final Answer
$$A = 25 \text{ square meters}$$

Problem 80

Area of Sector
Given
$r = 6$ feet, $\;\theta = 2$ radians.   Find $A$.
Step 1 — $A = \frac{1}{2}r^2\theta$
$$A = \frac{1}{2}(6)^2(2) = \frac{1}{2}(36)(2) = 36 \text{ ft}^2$$
Final Answer
$$A = 36 \text{ square feet}$$

Problem 81

Area of Sector
Given
$\theta = \dfrac{1}{4}$ radian, $\;A = 6$ square centimeters.   Find $r$.
Step 1 — Solve $A = \frac{1}{2}r^2\theta$ for $r$
$$r^2 = \frac{2A}{\theta}$$
Step 2 — Substitute
$$r^2 = \frac{2(6)}{1/4} = 12 \times 4 = 48$$
Step 3 — Take the positive square root ($r > 0$)
$$r = \sqrt{48} = 4\sqrt{3} \approx 6.928 \text{ cm}$$
Final Answer
$$r = 4\sqrt{3} \approx 6.928 \text{ centimeters}$$

Problem 82

Area of Sector
Given
$\theta = \dfrac{1}{3}$ radian, $\;A = 2$ square feet.   Find $r$.
Step 1 — $r^2 = 2A/\theta$
$$r^2 = \frac{2(2)}{1/3} = 4 \times 3 = 12$$
Step 2 — Square root
$$r = \sqrt{12} = 2\sqrt{3} \approx 3.464 \text{ ft}$$
Final Answer
$$r = 2\sqrt{3} \approx 3.464 \text{ feet}$$

Problem 83

Area of Sector
Given
$r = 6$ meters, $\;A = 8$ square meters.   Find $\theta$.
Step 1 — Solve $A = \frac{1}{2}r^2\theta$ for $\theta$
$$\theta = \frac{2A}{r^2}$$
Step 2 — Substitute
$$\theta = \frac{2(8)}{(6)^2} = \frac{16}{36} = \frac{4}{9} \approx 0.444 \text{ rad}$$
Final Answer
$$\theta = \frac{4}{9} \approx 0.444 \text{ radians}$$

Problem 84

Area of Sector
Given
$r = 5$ miles, $\;A = 3$ square miles.   Find $\theta$.
Step 1 — $\theta = 2A/r^2$
$$\theta = \frac{2(3)}{(5)^2} = \frac{6}{25} = 0.24 \text{ rad}$$
Final Answer
$$\theta = \frac{6}{25} = 0.240 \text{ radians}$$

Problem 85

Area of Sector — Degree Conversion
Given
$r = 3$ meters, $\;\theta = 120°$.   Find $A$.
Step 1 — Convert to radians
$$\theta = 120° \times \frac{\pi}{180} = \frac{2\pi}{3} \text{ rad}$$
Step 2 — $A = \frac{1}{2}r^2\theta$
$$A = \frac{1}{2}(3)^2 \cdot \frac{2\pi}{3} = \frac{1}{2}(9)\cdot\frac{2\pi}{3} = 3\pi \approx 9.425 \text{ m}^2$$
Final Answer
$$A = 3\pi \approx 9.425 \text{ square meters}$$
