★ Key Notes for this Section
Three Main Log Rules
Product Rule: $\log_a(MN) = \log_a M + \log_a N$ Quotient Rule: $\log_a\!\left(\dfrac{M}{N}\right) = \log_a M - \log_a N$ Power Rule: $\log_a(M^r) = r \log_a M$
Change of Base Formula
$\log_a M = \dfrac{\ln M}{\ln a} = \dfrac{\log M}{\log a}$ Use this to evaluate logs with any base on a calculator.
Common Mistakes to Avoid
• $\log(M+N) \neq \log M + \log N$ • $\log(M-N) \neq \log M - \log N$ • $\dfrac{\log M}{\log N} \neq \log M - \log N$ • $(\log M)^r \neq r \log M$   (power rule requires arg is raised, not log itself)

Problem 29

Expand Logarithm
Given
Write $\;\log_3(x^2 y)\;$ as a sum/difference of simpler logarithms.
Apply Product and Power Rules
$\log_3(x^2 y) = \log_3(x^2) + \log_3 y$ $= 2\log_3 x + \log_3 y$
Exam Answer
Apply Product and Power Rules
$\log_3(x^2 y) = \log_3(x^2) + \log_3 y$ $= 2\log_3 x + \log_3 y$
Final Answer
$2\log_3 x + \log_3 y$

Problem 56

Condense to Single Logarithm
Given
Write $\;\log_3(x^2-1) - \log_3(x-1)\;$ as a single logarithm.
Apply Quotient Rule and factor
$= \log_3\!\left(\dfrac{x^2-1}{x-1}\right) = \log_3\!\left(\dfrac{(x-1)(x+1)}{x-1}\right)$ $= \log_3(x+1)$   (for $x > 1$)
Exam Answer
Apply Quotient Rule and factor
$= \log_3\!\left(\dfrac{x^2-1}{x-1}\right) = \log_3\!\left(\dfrac{(x-1)(x+1)}{x-1}\right)$ $= \log_3(x+1)$   (for $x > 1$)
Final Answer
$\log_3(x+1)$
