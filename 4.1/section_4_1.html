Problem 37

Graphing with Transformations
Given
f(x) = (x + 2)^4 - 3
Step 1 — Write Down the Standard Form
Any polynomial of the form $f(x) = a(x-h)^n + k$ is a transformation of the parent $y = x^n$. For the given function, identify: - $a = 1$ (vertical scaling / reflection) - $h = -2$ (horizontal shift) - $n = 4$ (degree / shape) - $k = -3$ (vertical shift)
Step 2 — Identify the Parent Function
Remove $a$, $h$, and $k$ and look at the bare power: $$y = x^{4}$$ This is an even-degree power function. Its graph is U-shaped, symmetric about the y-axis, with a minimum at the origin.
Step 3 — List Every Transformation (Apply in Order)
Apply each transformation one at a time, in this order: reflect → stretch/compress → horizontal shift → vertical shift. 1. Shift 2 unit(s) to the left (factor is $(x + 2)$). 2. Shift 3 unit(s) down.
Step 4 — Locate the Key Point (Vertex)
The parent's key point is at $(0,\ 0)$. After the horizontal shift by $h = -2$ and vertical shift by $k = -3$: $$\text{Vertex} = (-2,\ -3)$$ This is the minimum of the graph.
Step 5 — End Behavior
As $x \to -\infty,\ f(x) \to +\infty$ and as $x \to +\infty,\ f(x) \to +\infty$ (graph rises on both sides, like $\cup$).
Step 6 — y-intercept
Substitute $x = 0$ into $f(x)$: $$f(0) = 1(0 - (-2))^{4} + -3 = 13$$ So the y-intercept is $(0,\ 13)$.
Step 7 — Sketch Notes
1. Plot the vertex at $(-2,\ -3)$. 2. Plot the y-intercept at $(0,\ 13)$. 3. Use the end-behavior arrows from Step 5. 4. The graph is symmetric about the vertical line $x = -2$.
Final Answer Parent: $y = x^{4}$. Vertex: $(-2,\ -3)$. y-intercept: $(0,\ 13)$. As $x \to -\infty,\ f(x) \to +\infty$ and as $x \to +\infty,\ f(x) \to +\infty$ (graph rises on both sides, like $\cup$).

Problem 43

Form a Polynomial
Given
Zeros: -1, 1, 3; degree 3
Step 1 — Recall the Factor Theorem
The Factor Theorem states: > If $r$ is a zero of polynomial $f(x)$, then $(x - r)$ is a factor. If $r$ has multiplicity $m$, then $(x - r)^m$ is a factor.
Step 2 — Write One Factor for Each Zero
From the given zeros, build the factors: - Zero $x = -1$ with multiplicity $1$ $\Rightarrow$ factor $((x + 1))$ - Zero $x = 1$ with multiplicity $1$ $\Rightarrow$ factor $((x - 1))$ - Zero $x = 3$ with multiplicity $1$ $\Rightarrow$ factor $((x - 3))$
Step 3 — Write in Factored Form (use $a = 1$)
With leading coefficient $a = 1$: $$f(x) = (x + 1) \cdot (x - 1) \cdot (x - 3)$$
Step 4 — Expand to Standard Form
Multiply out all factors (use FOIL / distributive property): $$f(x) = x^3 - 3x^2 - x + 3$$
Step 5 — Verify Degree
The highest power of $x$ in the expanded form gives the degree. Degree = 3 ✓
Final Answer $f(x) = (x + 1) \cdot (x - 1) \cdot (x - 3) = x^3 - 3x^2 - x + 3$

Problem 51

Polynomial Through a Point
Given
Zeros: -2, 0, 2 | Point: (-4, 16)
Step 1 — Write the General Form with Unknown Leading Coefficient
Since the zeros are fixed but the leading coefficient $a$ is unknown, write: $$f(x) = a \cdot (x + 2) \cdot x \cdot (x - 2)$$
Step 2 — Substitute the Given Point to Solve for $a$
The graph passes through $(-4,\ 16)$, so $f(-4) = 16$. Substitute $x = -4$ into the base (without $a$): $$\text{Base at } x=-4: \quad (x + 2) \cdot x \cdot (x - 2)\Big|_{x=-4} = -48$$ Therefore: $$a \cdot -48 = 16$$ $$a = \frac{16}{-48} = -1/3$$
Step 3 — Write the Final Factored Form
$$f(x) = -1/3((x + 2) \cdot x \cdot (x - 2))$$
Step 4 — Expand to Standard Form
$$f(x) = -x^3/3 + 4x/3$$
Step 5 — Verify
Check: substitute the given point $(-4,\ 16)$: $$f(-4) = -1/3 \cdot -48 = 16 \checkmark$$
Final Answer $f(x) = -1/3((x + 2) \cdot x \cdot (x - 2)) = -x^3/3 + 4x/3$
