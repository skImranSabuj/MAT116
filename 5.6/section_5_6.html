★ Key Notes for this Section
Solving Logarithmic Equations
1. State domain (argument of log must be $> 0$) 2. Combine logs on each side using log properties 3. Convert to exponential form: $\log_a M = c \Leftrightarrow M = a^c$ 4. Solve the resulting equation 5. Check all solutions against domain restrictions (reject extraneous)
Solving Exponential Equations — Substitution Method
For equations of the form $a^{2x} + b \cdot a^x + c = 0$: Let $u = a^x$, so $a^{2x} = u^2$ Solve the quadratic in $u$ Keep only $u > 0$ (since $a^x > 0$ always) Then solve $a^x = u$ for $x$
