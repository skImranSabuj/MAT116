★ Key Notes for this Section
Solving Logarithmic Equations
1. State domain (argument of log must be $> 0$) 2. Combine logs on each side using log properties 3. Convert to exponential form: $\log_a M = c \Leftrightarrow M = a^c$ 4. Solve the resulting equation 5. Check all solutions against domain restrictions (reject extraneous)
Solving Exponential Equations — Substitution Method
For equations of the form $a^{2x} + b \cdot a^x + c = 0$: Let $u = a^x$, so $a^{2x} = u^2$ Solve the quadratic in $u$ Keep only $u > 0$ (since $a^x > 0$ always) Then solve $a^x = u$ for $x$

Problem 15

Logarithmic Equation
Given
Solve: $\;2\log_3(x+4) - \log_3 9 = 2$
Step 1 — State domain restriction
Argument of each log must be positive. $x + 4 > 0 \Rightarrow x > -4$
Step 2 — Evaluate the known logarithm
$\log_3 9 = 2 \quad$ (since $3^2 = 9$) Substitute: $\;2\log_3(x+4) - 2 = 2$
Step 3 — Isolate the log (add 2 to both sides)
$2\log_3(x+4) = 4$
Step 4 — Remove the coefficient (divide both sides by 2)
$\log_3(x+4) = 2$
Step 5 — Convert to exponential form
Recall: $\log_b A = c \Longleftrightarrow A = b^c$ $x + 4 = 3^2 = 9$
Step 6 — Solve for x
$x = 9 - 4 = 5$
✅ Check — verify against domain
$x = 5 > -4$ ✓ Verify: $2\log_3(9) - \log_3 9 = 2(2) - 2 = 2$ ✓
💡 Mini Insight (Exam Shortcut)
Once you arrive at $\log_3(x+4) = 2$, recall $\log_3 9 = 2$, so immediately: $x + 4 = 9 \Rightarrow x = 5$ — no formal conversion needed!

Problem 17

Logarithmic Equation
Given
Solve: $\;\log x + \log(x-21) = 2$
Step 1 — State domain restriction
Both arguments must be positive: $x > 0$ and $x - 21 > 0$ Stricter condition: $x > 21$
Step 2 — Apply the Product Rule
Product Rule: $\log M + \log N = \log(MN)$ $\log[x(x-21)] = 2$
Step 3 — Convert to exponential form (base 10)
Recall: $\log A = c \Longleftrightarrow A = 10^c$ $x(x-21) = 10^2 = 100$
Step 4 — Expand and rearrange
$x^2 - 21x = 100$ $x^2 - 21x - 100 = 0$
Step 5 — Factor the quadratic
Find two numbers that multiply to $-100$ and add to $-21$: $-25$ and $+4$ $(x-25)(x+4) = 0$ $x = 25 \quad$ or $\quad x = -4$
✅ Check — reject extraneous solutions
Reject $x = -4$: fails domain ($-4 \not> 21$). Check $x = 25$: $\log 25 + \log 4 = \log(100) = 2$ ✓
💡 Mini Insight (Exam Shortcut)
When you get two solutions from a quadratic, always test both against the domain. Here, only $x = 25$ satisfies $x > 21$.

Problem 19

$2x > 0$ and $x - 3 > 0$ — stricter condition: $x > 3$

✅ Check — verify against domain
$x = \dfrac{15}{4} = 3.75 > 3$ ✓ Verify: $\log(7.5) - \log(0.75) = \log(10) = 1$ ✓
